MIT Launch

Program Website Self Check

This past summer (2016), I attended MIT Launch, a 4-week high school business incubator focused on teaching young entrepreneurs the essentials of launching startups. It took place on MIT's campus at Next Door.

Through the program, we walked through countless case studies, talks from MIT/Harvard Professors and students, and startup simulations. We conducted market research interviews in MIT cafeterias and "ideated" in the dorm hallways. Extensive resources were provided to us, including access to founder meetups, the MIT Trust Center for Entrepreneurship, and a ProtoWorks 3D Printing Lab.

I worked on an iOS application called SelfCheck, which allows users to conduct self-examinations and track any irregularities on their bodies.

Angles Speaker Event

Videos of Talks Promo Website

All coming from a competitive, fast paced pubic school, last year a few friends and I decided to set out to try and combat the one dimensional view of success that grades and numbers define everything about you. In order to do that, we brought in 5 speakers all with different backgrounds but one common theme: to introduce a new angle on life (hence the name, Angles: acute intersections of interesting perspectives).

We got over 200+ attendees to sign up and attend the event, in which we also held a Q/A afterwards with questions voted on by the attendees in between speeches and during breaks. Our school’s PTSA loved the idea, and gave us a grant to fund all expenses of renting the facility and getting presents for the speakers + goodie bags for the attendees.

Self Check

Website App Store Yale Pitch Night MIT Launch

Self Check is an iOS application which focuses on allowing users to remind themselves to conduct self examinations + track any irregularities they find on their body. In the future we plan on expanding with a feature to connect with local dermatologists and replace full on appointments with a cheaper fee of sending in photos.

We’ve been in contact with doctors from Harvard Medical School and dermatologists running their own clinics, all of which are excited about tele-dermatology rising in the industry and ready to help beta test our app. Incubated in 4 weeks at MIT Launch.

Classfinder

Website

The project that started it all for me. In 6th grade, I got together with my friends Matthew and Will to solve one of our largest headaches as 11 year olds: knowing who would be in our classes the upcoming year.

We built a drop dead simple web app that displayed your classes and who would be in them. After 5 years of design revisions and pushes every summer, we’ve amassed over 1000 users from 5 different schools in our area.

LHS Campaign

Website

An application me and my two friends built to make student council elections fair and equitable, allowing candidates to put up their platform, a picture, and links relevant to their campaigns— where the number of friends you have doesn’t define how much outreach you can get with your campaign.

We’ve been running it for 3 years, this year amassing 23,000+ page views from 1500 users. We also introduced a Q/A section into each candidate’s profile, which was extremely popular for engaging candidates and understanding their platforms. We’re currently working with our school administration to have our district officially sponsor and use the web app for all school elections.
